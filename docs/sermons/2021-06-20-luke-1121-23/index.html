And you have in this book an allegory. where people and places and things have a symbolic or hidden meaning. Now in this story, Diabolos, which is Satan, attacks the city of man’s soul, which is the soul of man. And he occupies the castle in the city, which is the heart of man. And he rules of the city. over the city, which is the man himself. King Shaddai, which comes from the Hebrew L. Shadai, speaking about Almighty God. Almighty God resents this move by Diabolus and has a gracious intention of free man’s soul and rescue it. Diabolus fortifies the city best as he can, and he plans to fight to retain it. King Shadai sends his beloved son, Emmanuel, which it means God with us, which speaks, of course, of Jesus Christ. To conquer man’s soul, and Emmanuel proves to be too strong for Diabolus to resist. So Emmanuel defeats. So diabolis, liberates all the citizens of Man’s Soul, and they rejoice in the rescue. Now, this whole story, the Holy War, is really based on this text of Scripture that we have before us this morning. And you can see it. We have here the Lord Jesus Christ who has ever in conflict with Satan. And whenever he strikes a blow against Satan, it is meant ultimately to lead to Satan’s destruction. For this purpose, the Son of God was manifested that he might destroy the works of the devil. We read in the book of 1 John 3 in verse 8. So there’s this eternal hatred. towards Satan. Where Christ healed was bruised on the cross, he’s going to have a fatal blow against the head of the serpent, Satan himself. So there’s nothing more absurd. than the foolish assertion of the Pharisees that Jesus is casting out demons by the power of Beelzebub, the prince of the devils or Satan himself. What could be more incongruous with Christ’s work than that? Satan never works against himself or he would undermine his own kingdom. He does. He does all he can to hold on to what he has. It was the finger of God by which Christ was doing this. It was the power of God by which he was casting out these demons, and that should be clearly recognized. So our topic this morning is really the strong man overcome, or, if you prefer, the Holy War. We see, first of all, the power of Satan over your unconverted soul here in verse 21. It just shows you that no matter how you argue and how even the best arguments that we could muster to deal with the hearts of men, they will not be moved by these things in and of themselves. Satan is determined to maintain his hold over you, and you see that Satan keeps his goods in peace, it says here. Now, there is a kind of peace that the unconverted people have. And that is that they are asleep to the danger of their condition. They’re not really aware of how serious their condition really is. They think themselves to be pretty good people. Maybe they go to church on Sundays. They’re kind of their neighbor. And so they don’t see themselves as in grave danger. They don’t have any idea of Satan’s dominion over their souls. You say to your soul, well, take your ease, eat and drink, be merry, enjoy life while you’re here. And that’s the way people live their lives. So you don’t see yourself as Satan’s servant. You blissfully pass your time here on earth in ignorance of that fact. Satan however says, I will have his mouth and he shall swear for me. I shall have his eyes, and they shall wonder after vanity. I shall have his feet. They shall work for me and be my slave. So the natural man is not usually troubled unless the word of God comes with conviction upon him. But he’s at peace in his situation, so he’s not trying to escape Satan’s dominion over him. He’s comfortable the way he is. So you see Satan keeps his goods in peace. Now, let me make this observation on this first main point. You should never think lightly of Satan. This passage teaches us that even though people commonly jest about Satan and don’t take him seriously, that’s foolish. It is a great evil. He is very strong, the Bible says. He’s alive and well in this world. He goes about as a roaring lion, seeking whom he may devire, and he’s a strong man. He has power over men’s souls. He’s determined to maintain the control over man. So man’s in a very dangerous position and doesn’t even know it. When you witness for Christ, Satan is opposing every word you say. to that person. This is why it’s so difficult in dealing with those who are blind to the things of God, because Satan will try to keep them blind. He is snatching away the seed of the word you were sowing, doing all he can to prevent the heart from being affected by the word of God. And without divine intervention, all are witnessing, all are witnessing, all are people. persuading will be in vain. How we need to pray for God to cast out the devil. How we need to pray that God would break through to the heart, to the palace of man’s soul, and that they may embrace the gospel. Satan is a formidable foe to Christ’s kingdom. And you would do well not to underestimate him. And so the power of Satan over your unconverted soul, as mentioned here. But then secondly, we see the supreme power of Christ overcoming Satan’s dominion over your soul. In verses 22 and 23. But the Son of God possesses all power on heaven and earth. He cannot be thwarted. There is no contest. In Revelation, you recall, it is Christ that lays hold upon the dragon, you remember, Satan, and cast him into the bottom of his pit. Effortlessly, he does so. With ease. And he does so that the door of the gospel would be open to the Gentiles, you recall, that the gospel could go. off into the world and people which could be converted from all nations. It’s interesting that John the Baptist called Jesus Christ the mighty one using the very sane Greek words here in our text. He says, one mightier than I, or the mighty one, the mightier one than I. So you are assured that Jesus Christ is stronger than Satan. You find here that Jesus Christ assaults Satan and overcomes him. He shall come upon him and overcome him, the Bible says. Jesus Christ attacks Satan, and whenever he comes and attacks Satan, he overwhelms him. As a consequence of his superior strength, he gains the victory, and he frees his people. Satan has confidence in his armor, but Jesus Christ is not going to be thwarted. Satan has no chance to withstand Christ. Psalm 45 verses this. Gird thy sword upon thy thigh, O mighty one, almost mighty. With thy glory and thy majesty, and in thy majesty ride prosperously. Speaking of Jesus Christ. Going forth to conquer. Does the devil strive to keep Christ out of the hearts of man? Of course he does. Does he trust in his weapons of ignorance to hold his subjects? Yes, he does. But Christ causes a beam of light to break through the darkness. Does Satan try to hold his subjects by doctrinal error? Christ causes them to know the truth. Satan tries to keep his subjects in unbelief, but Christ bestows the grace of faith and destroys that effort. Ultimately, Satan is foiled. Jesus Christ prevails, and he takes possession of the citadel of men’s hearts, the palace of men. So you find Jesus Christ assaults Satan and overcomes him. You find that Jesus Christ divides the spoils here. When Christ takes possession of the heart, he now controls all the endowments that a man has. the mind and the body of the individual. So that his thinking, his affections, his will, his understanding, his eyes, his ears, his feet are all now Christ’s possessions. And he uses them to honor and glorify himself. So there’s a new application of man’s faculty. and powers when Christ converts the soul. Now they are used to advance Christ’s kingdom on earth. He causes those conquered to use their faculties and powers that benefit their fellow believers. You find Jesus Christ divides the spoils. Now notice Christ concludes with this thought. You can’t remain neutral in this conflict between Christ and Satan. Notice verse 23. He that is not with me is against me. He that gathers not with me, scabbardth. If you’re not for Christ, you must be against him. Now here Christ speaks of those who dismissed his miracles, who rejected his God. They refuse to follow Christ as Lord and Savior. They will not commit themselves to Christ. Oh, you may have some respect for Christ, for you are a religious person, but you prefer an easy religion. You can’t tolerate the strictness of what is required in the following of the narrow way. of true Christianity. You prefer a lukewarm religion. You don’t want to be cold, and you don’t want to be hot either. So you prefer the lukewarmness of the middle ground. You halt between two opinions. You wish you could avoid being looked upon as a heathen. You don’t want to be looked upon that way. But you don’t wish to be too serious about religions. So you’re lukewarm. If this describes you, then Jesus says, you’re really his enemy. You’re on Satan’s side. You’re not gathering into his kingdom. You’re not working with him. You’re really working against him.
