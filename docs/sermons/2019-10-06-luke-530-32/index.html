The third thing we see here this morning is the necessity of Christ dealing with publicans and sinners in verse 31. Jesus’ answering said unto them, they that are whole need not a physician, but they that are sick. I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance. Now, let me simply say this. There’s no one who is really whole or righteous. You must realize that. All have need of the great physician, but many people do not recognize this truth. They deny that they are sick. The scribes and the Pharisees consider themselves spiritually healthy. They consider themselves righteous. They did not see the need of Christ at all. So Christ leaves them alone. I came to take care of the sick. Accknowledge sinners. If you took a glass of water and you put some dirt in it and then you stirred it up and made it muddy water. And you let that glass sit. Eventually, the dirt debris would all slowly settle down to the bottom of the water. And if you looked at the glass after a while, it would appear to be fairly clean at the top in the middle. You could see through it. This is the way. way sinners are. The simple stirring of that water, however, brings it back to its state of mud. You know, it’s all dirty again if you just stir it up. The heart of man is full of the dirt, of sinful lusts, and carnal desires. However, when those evil lust and desires are not stirred up, you might imagine that your heart is pretty clean. You might love you, say, I’m a pretty good guy. you know, I don’t have any major sins in my life and so forth. So you might think, I’m righteous. I’m good. I don’t need a savior. Believe that your nature is evil and depraved as the scripture says it is. However, to be proved wrong, all you need is for a lust to be incited in your life, to stir it up. and you’ll see how wicked you are and what you’re capable of doing. I remind you, the scribes and the Pharisees are the one that are plotting murder against the Lord Jesus’ grace. Don’t tell me about how righteous you are. Why you want to murder people. Why out of covetousness you take advantage of widows, take their money from them and so forth. Don’t tell me about your righteousness. You’re not righteous at all. So you must realize nobody is really whole or righteous. But you need to realize that you’re spiritually sick. Now, sin is the sickness of the soul, not the body, but the soul. It is a deforming, weakening, wasting, and killing disease. The picture of the soul. The picture of a deforming, weakening, sin as a disease really doesn’t portray to us all the heinous nature of sin. It’s just a way for us to get a handle on how bad sin is. The idea of sickness doesn’t capture perhaps the perverseness of the will, or the evil rebelliousness of man’s nature, or the enmity and hatred of man toward God. But it’s just a way to view sin from a different perspective and get a handle on it. Think about this. Sin is an hereditary disease. You’re born in sin. You’re shaping in iniquity. And sin did your mother conceive you? For Adam’s original transgression is conferred to you. So it’s hereditary. It’s also a disabling disease. Those who are spiritually sick cannot please God. You can’t do good. You’re incapable of seeking after God, of praying and worshiping God right, of understanding the ways of God, or exhibiting any spiritual strength towards your salvation. It is a polluting disease. You defile everything you touch. If you’re evil, you make others around you evil. Your children imitate your vices. You influence your family, your friends, your neighbors, your coworkers by your behavior. It’s polluting. It’s an incurable disease. Can the Ethiopian change his skin or the leopard, his spots, that you are accustomed to do evil, not learn to do well. You can’t heal yourself. You can’t cure yourself. You can’t overcome this disease yourself. And it’s a fatal disease. But the wages of sin is death. Not merely bodily death, but eternal death in hellfire. So you are indeed suffering soul sickness and will utterly perish without divine intervention. that’s at least what we can gain from this. So you need to realize you are spiritually sick. Now, Christ is the physician for the spiritually sick. That’s what he tells us here. He specializes in it. He is the only one who can cure your dreaded disease. Jesus didn’t come into the world to save the righteous or the so-called righteous, but sinners. We were. He’s written, scripture, that Christ died for sinners. That Christ makes intercession for what? The transgressors. So while here on earth, he spends his time with known sinners, or at least acknowledge sinners, for they need him. Do you see your soul is sick? Do you see yourself as a sinner in the eyes of God? Do you desire to be healed? Then you go to the great physician. Man’s natural ignorance and self-righteousness and religion is such that men lose sight of the fact that they need Christ, the great physician. Christ came to call you to repentance, to turn you from your sins. He has to deliver sinners by dying in their place as a substitute and paying the debt that they owe. for the penalty of the law. He has to place your trust in Christ because he can wash away all your sins. He can cure this dreaded disease. So we see the necessity of Christ dealing with public and sinners. So his answer to the scribes and Pharisees is, what do you do when he eat, what do you do in eating with publicans? They need me. They need the Savior. You may not think you need me, but they know they need me. I always said, It’s always better to deal with a person who knows their Republican and sinner than to deal with one who’s a Pharisee. You’re always better off to deal with someone who acknowledges their sinner than the ones who think they’re self-righteous, because how can you reach the self-righteous? George Washington, though General of the Continental Army, was willing to join in and help his common soldiers move a log into place. Our Savior did not just let men struggle helplessly in their sin, but he stepped in and saved sinners from their sin. So we learned from this passage that Christ is willing to receive sinners. We learned from this passage that Christ, it’s common for his good actions to be maligned, to be evil spoken of. We learned from this passage that nobody is truly whole or righteous in the eyes of God. We learn from this passage that Christ is the only physician for soul sickness.
