God blessed to us His sacred and holy word this morning. now believer is in some ways like a butterfly we come into this world like a caterpillar an ugly one at that would depraved and corrupt and alienated from God our life is one of crawling around with regard to being attached to the things of this earth but then we undergo a God-given renewal, a spiritual change. Just as a caterpillar enters a cancun, there’s a sense in which we have to die to ourselves, and then we find that we emerge as a beautiful butterfly, able to assure above the things of this world with a new identity and a new character. Obviously, a butterfly can’t go back to living as a caterpillar. He’s undergone a dramatic change and is an entirely different creature. And so you as a believer can no longer live as you used to live. As an unregenerate person, you’ve undergone a dramatic change as someone who has been regenerated by Jesus Christ. The word, therefore, in verse 12, connects this verse with what Paul has just taught. He has spoken about putting off those things associated with your old man in verses 8 and 9. He now speaks about putting on those virtues which are associated with your new man, which he spoke about in verse 10. Paul first speaks about your new identity and then he addresses your new character. what you should exhibit in your life. Let me begin with this first thought. You’ve been given a new identity as a believer. You notice he says here, you are one of God’s elect people as the elect of God. Once again, divine election is found in Epistle of Paul. It all frequently comes up. It is frequently emphasized by the apostle as a doctrine of importance for your life. Sadly, some professing Christians would prefer we never mention election. As if it were a dirty word that should never be talked about, a taboo subject. We should avoid it. How different the attitude of the apostle Paul, who sees divine election as an incentive to live a godly life. Scripture teaches that God has a people which were chosen in Christ from eternity according to God’s sovereign will in good pleasure as we read in Ephesians chapter 1 verses 3 to 5. If you are one of God’s elect people, you were chosen under salvation and eternal life. And in time, you were drawn to Christ by the irresistible power of God. So your salvation is not based upon your will or your works, but is solely of God’s free grace. Paul, by a judgment of charity here, assumes that all here who are professing Christ and are members of the Church of Colossé are indeed the elect of God. There may be some hypocrites among them, but for the majority of them, they indeed are the elect of God. Now, your knowledge of election here is not meant to promote profane living. Some people tell us, well, if you teach election and the security of the believer, then, of course, they are going to go out and live an evil life. But you’ll notice that Paul uses the word election. election in this text in connection with putting on the new man.
