In France, there once lived a poor blind girl who managed to obtain a gospel of Mark in Braille and she learned to read it with her fingers, the tips of her fingers. And by constant reading, however, her fingers became calloused and she was having more and more difficult reading. Now, Paul here has been addressing the qualifications of elders of the church. And now we come to another branch of the elders’ fitness for this office. He must be a man of the word. Holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost. And that’s true, not only of the Old Testament, but of the new as well. As Paul declared to Timothy, all scripture is given by inspiration of God. It’s God breathed. The Holy Spirit is the actual author of the scripture. And because it is the inspired, infallible word, it is trustworthy. It is can be depended upon. It is truth. It is the absolute unchanging truth of God. All its doctrines of true, all its promises will come to pass, and since God is faithful, his word must be faithful also. His word resembles him in many ways. Is God omnipotent? But the Word of God has been preserved in all ages as something we must believe, so that what we have today, we can have have confidence that we are giving out the Word of God and it hasn’t been lost and hasn’t been somehow compromised in the meantime. And man’s traditions could be put on the level with the word. of God. No. There’s only one faithful word and that’s God’s word. What’s interesting about this class of people that we see is that they have turned away from Sola Scriptora in many ways. His point was God isn’t speaking audibly to anybody anymore. The Word of God is complete. It’s all that’s necessary for salvation, for faith, and for life. As the London Baptist Confession brightly stated, those former ways of God revealing his will are now ceased. We don’t have Christ walking the earth, apostles, or prophets today. Why? An elder must believe that the Holy Scripture is the only sufficient, certain, and infallible rule of all saving knowledge, faith, and obedience. So an elder must believe in the trustworthiness of the word of God. The second point I would make tonight is this. An elder must cling tenaciously to the truth of the word of God. He tells us here that the elder must hold fast the faithful word. He must know and be convinced of the teaching of Scripture. In the middle voice, as it often occurs here, as it occurs here, I should say, this verb hold fast can mean to hold oneself face to face with, which means to draw near or to approach closely with. And so one of the first ideas, I suggest to you, associated with the word hold fast, is to lay hold of the truth, to grasp it, to know it well, to meditate in it day and night. This is an extremely important command, is it not, for an elder of the church. He must know the teachings of the Word of God thoroughly. He must learn them in such a way that he has a clear grasp of the doctrines of the Christian faith. He cannot be someone who wavers with every wind of doctrine that blows along. He must show himself to be approved under God, a workman, that needeth not to be ashamed that rightly dividing the Word of Truth. Someone who is labors in the Word, to understand it and know it, you can’t be unsteady, you can’t be unsure of its teachings. We have to have a systematic grasp of the truth of Scripture. We ought to be able to compare Scripture with Scripture, so that what Scripture says in one place, it matches up with what it says somewhere else, and perhaps it gives us more enlightenment on that particular passage that we’re trying to understand. Now, coming to know the truth of Scripture, an elder must believe it, be convinced of it with all his heart. The study of Scripture should not be an academic exercise. It’s a lifetime of growing in the knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ because God speaks through His Word. The study of Scripture then becomes the way in which God speaks to us that we may speak to others. So remember, an elder is supposed to be an example in this regard. It’s not that other believers shouldn’t be studying the Word of God day and night as well, but he is to set the example. As a teacher of the church, he has a greater responsibility in this regard. So he must know and be convinced of the teaching of Scripture. He must grab hold of it, so to speak. But he must tenaciously adhere to God’s truth. To hold fast not only means to hold one’s self-face-to-face with, but it implies sort of a cleaving, a clinging, a holding tightly to the Scripture, the faithful word. Thus the verb properly signifies such holding of the truth that one will not let it be torn from them. Think about a woman who’s going through a dangerous section of town and she has her purse and she’s clinging to it. Not going to allow anybody to steal it from her. Snatch it away from her. She’s going to fight to keep it. An elder, must lay hold on the truth in such a way that he’s not going to allow it to be wrestled from him. He has a firm grip on it. He contends for the faith, once delivered under the saints. That’s what the Scripture would indicate to us. Today, there’s pressure to let go of the Holy Scriptures. It comes from a variety of directions, in particular our society is much, involved in pluralism. By that I mean the idea that all religions are really equal. We can all come together. We can all worship. We’re all worshipping the same God kind of idea. Various religious leaders accept the concept, although we’re all children of God, even though we may differ in doctrine and belief. But to come to that point, one must lose their grip on the faithful word, because that is not what God’s word says. The Bible presents only one way of salvation, and that’s through Jesus Christ and his righteousness and his sacrifice. That’s it. G.E. Now they weren’t trying to figure out, well, which one is the genuine wing? No one can be sure which religion is true, so don’t push your own on others. Do not be too worried about the truth. Faithful elder isn’t going to swallow that lie. God doesn’t lie, and he doesn’t make any imitation rings either. False religions, there we find that people hold doctrines of devils. Don’t tell me that we’re all worshipping the same God. We don’t need your bloody religion. An elder must be a man who holds the trustworthy message and will not let the church be robbed of it by flattering words, or fashionable arguments. This often leads to sacrifice and hardship on the part of the church. It may lead the ridicule and ostracism. Be thankful we have such men who will not give up. They will cling to the truth. My third point tonight is this. An elder must carefully maintain the message of the word of God as originally given. You’ll notice he has the words here, as he hath been taught, Paul underlines the carefulness with which elders must handle the gospel so that it’s not compromised. An elder must hold firmly to the message as he has been taught. Who’s taught him? Paul. Who’s taught him? Here’s another appointed attack on the gospel that we must pay attention with regard. there are those who say believe the truth of Scripture. Of course, we as elders, as we come to the Word of God, we’re going to put things in the modern context. The principles and truths of the Word of God, the propositions of Scripture, are always the same. They’re not going to change.
