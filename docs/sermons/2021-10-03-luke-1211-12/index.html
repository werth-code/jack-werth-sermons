In song, that you may sing his praise as you sing the songs of Zion. So don’t ignore Christ’s command but eagerly do what he says here. Let me see thy countenance. Let me hear thy voice. The dove’s consolation is this. For sweet is thy voice and thy countenance, he says, is commonly. There is no voice as sweet to Christ as that of his bride who pleads with him in prayer. There is no music as precious to Christ as the songs of Zion, sung by the people of God. Who is more lovely and beautiful as a soul that’s been washed in the blood of Christ and given his righteousness. There’s nobody to compare to that. Don’t hide. Come near. Draw near. I want you to come near. Now with that in mind, hear Christ’s command, do this and remembrance of me. Come to the table. Do this. And remember, don’t remain at a distance. Draw a near to the Savior. You may be like a dove. You may be fearful. You may be timid. You may be mournful. You may be at a distance. But Christ still declares here that you’re my dove. You’re beautiful. And your blames. As one saved by his grace. And the Lord’s supper reminds you, I have one. I am one of his redeemed people. Let’s see. Let’s keep that in mind when we come to the table. Lastly, see how tender Christ is toward a fainting believer. The Lord Jesus Christ desires to fellowship with you. Let me see thy comments. We should desire his fellowship. We should seek it. He’s not saying stay away. He’s saying come. Let’s fellowship together. And so he encourages us as people to come to the table. And I encourage you this morning. I hope that you’ve confessed all your known sins, and that you can come to the table with a heart that’s open to have Christ’s blessing, rest upon you this morning. Well, let’s have a word of prayer as we come to the table this morning. Heavenly Father, we thank you for the opportunity. we have the fellowship around your table. Bless your word to us. Encourage us as we come. And may we see, Father, that you love us. Yes, we are unworthy of the least mercy from you. We are unworthy of your love. But you’ll love us anyways. And I pray, Lord, that we may rest upon that as we come to the table this morning. and realize what a privilege it is to know you and the salvation that is in you. What’s in Jesus’ name, we pray these things. We ask for your blessing, Father, upon the bread and the cup this morning as we come to the table. Amen. As Mark come at this time, and we will have an opportunity to have the Lord’s supper. Now, we have… have the cup and the bread, both in little cups. So take one of each, if you would.
