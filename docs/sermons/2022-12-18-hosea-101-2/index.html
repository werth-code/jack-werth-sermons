So as a professing believer, you are expected to be spiritually fruitful. Secondly, why you may prove to be an empty vine. You may be selfishly using God’s gifts. In verse one, we see that God says you bore fruit here. He says, Israel is an empty vine. He bring it forth fruit unto himself. According to the multitude of his fruit, he hath increased the altars, according to the goodness of his land. They have made goodly images. Israel bore some type of fruit, but it wasn’t fruit unto God. Israel aimed exclusively to please herself. It should be noted that this was true with regard to worship and religious activities. For all seek their own, he says, and not the things that are Jesus Christ. Even among those claiming to be Christians all find selfish approach to life at times. False professors are empty because they are selfish. If you complain of fruitlessness, here is the reason. You’re selfish. People going through the motions of doing one thing, but actually they are intent on doing something else. Do you have that today? Of course you do. It’s a warning here against hypocrisy. Israel had purported love for God, but she was unfaithful. You find the emphasis here on adulterous worship. They were worshipping idols. The whole background of the book about Jose marriage comes into view here. No doubt when Josea’s wife Gomer went off with her lovers, she might still have said that she loved Josea, but love like that is no place in marriage. By its very nature, by the law of God, marriage is supposed to be an exclusive affair. It’s one man or one woman. Faithfulness to each other. In plenty or in want, in joy and sorrow and sickness and health till death harts you. When another comes along and enters into that marriage relationship, love is betrayed. And there’s a vacillation there. And we would say that the partner who strays is unfaithful. Israel was guilty of such a sin. They worship God, but they also worshiped idols. and therefore were unfaithful. She pretended to acknowledge Jehovah. She was multiplying all these false altars and dedicating sacred stones. She would have her lovers, even though by covenant she was supposed to be exclusively the Lord’s. She was committing spiritual adultery. Today there are so-called millions of Christians who do the same thing. They try to serve two masters, God and Manon. You would never think of denying the existence of the true God, perhaps. You might even claim that you’re one of his people and that you worship him. I go to church. I’m not a Buddhist. I’m not a Muslim. I’m not a Hindu. Yet, instead of worshipping God, many people are actually in. church because of what other people might think of them, or maybe to make a good impression. You need to make sure that you do not have a divided heart. Would you not merely say that you love God, that you actually do love him and serve him? A chief symptom of a divided heart is formality in religious worship. People stand. for form and lack the power of God. You’re a person on Sunday and you go to church and you appear to be a saint, and perhaps you can talk using the right religious language, nod your head at the proper moments in the sermon, sing the songs of praise, but you don’t call upon him any time during the rest of the week. For if people would look at your life, you’re not living any different than a worldling around you. You have a divided heart. It would be very pleasant circumstance if a minister could believe that everybody in his church was truly converted. Sadly, the larger the church, the less likely everyone is converted. But friends, there may be some of you who practice sins unseen by your pastor’s eye. You can be a very clever hypocrite that you’re not going to fool God. So why you may prove to be an empty vine? You’re selfishly using God’s good gifts and you have a divided heart. What God will do with you as an empty vine? The worship of Bethel was sinful. It was filled with heathen customs. and practices. And yet the people were basking in the good gifts of God enjoying his temporal favors. And we see that very clearly in the life of Israel. They kept building altars and monuments, but they did not repent. They refused to devote their hearts fully to the Lord. What would God want with all of them? if his people did not offer their very lives as living sacrifices to him. Why would God care about the restoration of monuments bearing images from the past if you don’t bother restoring God’s image in yourself? Thus, the Almighty will sweep away all the altars and destroy all the monuments. Serving God isn’t. quite as easy as it appears. You should not be so foolish as to think that you can deceive God with pomp and ceremony. God sees through it all. God will weigh you in his balances and you will be found wanting. He knows all about you. He will find you guilty. Friends, what does God care about beautiful church buildings? elaborate ceremonies, professional religious music in the services if his people are not living monuments to God’s faithfulness. God will take a broken and contrite part as a sacrifice. Hearts devoted to serving Christ, they are living monuments to His grace. But what will happen to empty vines in the end? God, the husbandmen, will examine you, for spiritual fruitfulness, and finding none he will cast you forth the wither in this life, and at the last day you will be cast into eternal fire and be burned according to John 15. This is a horrible fate, but it is the end that God has in store for all those who are spiritually empty.
