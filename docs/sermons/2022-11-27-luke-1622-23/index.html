Our text that I want to focus on this morning, verses 22 and 23, take up the issue of what happens when you die. And that’s… where our focus is this morning. Let me begin by saying this. You should realize death is universal. We look here at verse 22 and we read these words, and it came to pass that the beggar died and was carried by the angels into Abraham’s bosom. No doubt he had. had a pompous funeral with a train of mourners, a funeral oration, and praise of him. Probably a stately monument was set over his grave, something which would cause others to remember him in the years to come. But the key fact here is this. Everyone dies. There is no escaping such an end outside of the return of Christ. just as you had a day of birth, you will most certainly have a day of death. The Bible teaches and Adam all die. meaning that Adam’s fall had a tragic effect upon the whole human race which came from his loins. James 2.26. Physical death then can simply be stated in this way. It’s separation of the body and the soul. One of the clearest examples of this is found in the death of Rachel, the wife of Jacob, who died, you recall, who were in childbirth. And the scripture says it came to pass, as her soul was departing, for she died, that she called out. his name Benoni, son of Mar Sarah. And so while the body like that of the rich man is buried, his soul now separated from the body, continues to exist. You recall the death of Jesus on the cross was pinpointed. as the moment he gave up the ghost. His soul separated from his body. While his body was buried, his human soul continued to exist. You’ll recall what he said to one of the thieves on the cross, Today, thou shall be with me in paradise. So while his body is in the tomb, Jesus’ spirit goes to paradise. Christ made it clear there is conscious existence after death in this parable. Neither the person of the rich man or that of Lazarus was annihilated when they died. The Greek philosopher Aristotle, who died in 322 BC, said that death was the most to be feared of all things, for it appears to be the end of everything. A lot of people think that way. It’s the end of everything. No, it is not the end of everything. What man forgets is that unending existence is an inescapable part of man’s heritage as a creature made after the image and likeness of God. We read in Ecclesiastes chapter 12. Then shall the dust or the body of a man return to the earth as it was from whence it was created. And the spirit shall return unto God who gave it. When Jesus talked to the Sadducees about the resurrection of the dead, which they denied, you recall we refer to God identifying himself to Moses at the burning bush. As I am. the God of thy father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. And as Jesus declared, he’s not the God of the dead, but of the living, for all live unto him. In other words, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob still existed. So you’re not annihilated at death. You’re not ceased to exist when your body is very, in the ground, you have an immortal soul. The Bible nowhere speaks of your soul, falling asleep at death, and you existing in an unconscious state. Both rich man and Lazarus were conscious, fully conscious after death. So your soul exists separate from your body. Notice that your soul upon death will reside in either one of two places, heaven or heaven. hell. Let’s look at heaven for a moment. When at last day, where he had of Lazarus fell into the gutter in the silence of death, the holy angels came and carried his soul to Abraham’s bosom, the scripture says. Jesus makes use of this expression, the bosom of Abraham, the bosom of Abraham, For Abraham was the father of the faithful. And this figurative expression was meant to convey the fact that the souls of the righteous were gathered to join their father Abraham, the friend of God. Lazarus is pictured here as being welcomed into the family of heaven. He is carried to Abraham’s bosom. And this implies that the sorrows and troubles of Lazarus are now over. During feast, you know, guests are sometimes said to lean on the breast of others, like John on Christ’s breast at the Lord’s Supper. Being with Abraham after death implied happiness. It’s similar to that expression that we’re going to sit down with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, in the kingdom of God, Matthew 8. 11. In heaven, all tears are wiped from your eyes. There is no more sorrow, no more sickness, or suffering. Peace and joy prevail in the presence of God. To be absent from the body and to be present with the Lord is the joy of the Christian. In thy presence is fullness of joy and at thy right hand there are pleasures evermore. Psalm 1611. What affliction does, however, is teaching that it’s useless to seek happiness in this world. All Lazarus is suffering, his worldly portion of this life, caused him to seek something beyond this life. Lazarus being unsatisfied. feeling that temporal things should never satisfy. Affliction is like a talent that’s given to you by God, and you’re supposed to make something of it. You’re supposed to be benefited and gained by it. Sorrow, sickness, trial, poverty, bereavement should cause you to realize happiness is not found in this world. You begin to look heavenward. You seek those things which are above. You find in the promises of God’s word your peace and joy. So that in the end, you proclaim with the psalmist, it was very good. good for me that I was afflicted. Lazarus was much better off being afflicted than the rich man was, being surrounded by wealth and pleasures and worldly goods. Are you driven by your earthly suffering to seek satisfaction in the things of the Lord? Don’t you realize this is the good which can come out of all affliction? Your present suffering should make you look beyond this world. Notice you should consider the value of time. What is the length of your earthly life? Well, the psalma says maybe 70, maybe 80 years.
