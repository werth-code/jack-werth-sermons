May God bless to us His sacred and holy word this morning. Martin Luther was once told about a nobleman who, above all things, was occupied with making money. He was so obsessed with this pursuit that he refused to pay any attention to the Word of God. When someone pleaded with him to consider the state of his soul, he had the audacity to reply, sir, the gospel pays no interest. In addressing this, Luther gave this parable, or this fable. He said, a lion, making a great feast, invited all the beasts of the field to come along with some swine. And when all manner of delicious food was set before the guests, the swine asked, where’s the grain? And Luther explained, even so it is with carnal men. We preachers set before them the most dainty and costly dishes, such as everlasting salvation, the remission of sins in God’s grace. They like swine, turn up their snouts and ask for money. Now, friends, this sort of captures the difference between the worldling and the Christian. The Christian is seeking heavenly things, as verse one tells us, whereas the worldling confines himself to the things, the carnal things of this earth. as verse 2 indicates to us, you appreciate the value of heavenly things as a believer, whereas the worldling like swine trample under foot the pearls that are set before them.
