Luke chapter 2, beginning with verse 15 and reading through verse 20. And it came to pass as the angels were gone away from them into heaven. May God bless to us His sacred and holy word this morning. A minister was once attempting to demonstrate to his congregation the ignorance of many people with regard to the scriptures. and he said that someone once asked, what are the epistles, and the answer he received, they are the wives of the apostles. So the minister went on to say, this shows how little people know about the Bible. After the service, a woman came up to him and asked, Pastor, I don’t understand. If they weren’t the wives of the apostles, whose wives were they? Scripture relates that very few people were looking for Christ coming in the days of Joseph and Mary. And being born at a stable in Bethlehem was not where you would expect to find the Messiah. The spiritual ignorance which existed left almost everybody in the dark about Christ coming. But God will not leave the people in total ignorance. And so we find he sends an angel to announce the birth of Christ to the shepherds in the fields nearby Bethlehem. The shepherds investigate the divine message. And then they spread the news to many others about Christ’s birth. In this, in other ways, because we read about the Magi in the book of the Bible, in the book of of Matthew, we find that the coming of Christ becomes known, becomes more publicly recognized. But this morning I want to speak on the effect of the word of God from this passage of Scripture. Let me begin by saying this. You should promptly obey God’s Word. You’ll notice in verses 15 and 16, and it came to pass as the angels were gone away. from them into heaven. The shepherd said one to another, let us now go unto Bethlehem and see this thing which has come to pass, which the Lord hath made known to us, and they came with haste, and found Mary and Joseph and the babe lying in the manger. As we look at this passage of Scripture, the thing I think we should learn is this. You should immediately respond to God’s revealed word. After our Bible, brief consultation. The shepherds were resolved to go to Bethlehem. Now remember, in verse 12, they had been given a sign, as it says here, this shall be a sign unto you, shall find the bay wrapped in swatting clothes, lying in a manger, which indicates to us that the idea was that they should seek out the babe, the Christ, that was born. And so we see the response to the angel’s message is to immediately go forth, and the Bible tells us with haste they did it to find the coming Christ. They believed God’s word from the angels, from the angel here, and were determined to see this thing which has come to pass. And they didn’t hesitate. They didn’t delay till morning, but they proceeded immediately to the city of Bethlehem. Now, they were eventually able to locate Joseph and Mary and the Christ child, and they found everything exactly as the angel described it. They were convinced that they had beheld the Lord’s Christ. Their faith was fixed on the Savior, and this conveys, I think, an important message to us. Instead of allowing the Word of God to pass up, away quickly from your mind if you’ve heard it, apply it immediately. They acted on the message that they had received. It deserves your attention and your obedience. It is the word of God himself. And you should make haste to follow it. This truth has been revealed to you for a reason. You remember one day, the Lord gave Abraham a startling command. Take thy son, thine only son, you know the one you’ve been waiting for for all these years and you finally had, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Mariah and offer him there for a burnt sacrifice. Now, what’s interesting here is Abraham does not debate with the Lord about the propriety of sacrificing his son. He doesn’t try to argue with the Lord about this. What you do read is that Abraham rose up early in the morning and proceeded immediately to carry out God’s revealed will. In this respect, he’s a good example for you and I, for he quickly applied the word of God that he received. Duke of Wellington once replied to an officer of his engineers who urged the impossibility of executing the commander’s order. Sir, I did not ask your opinion. I gave you my orders, and I expect them to be obeyed. God doesn’t ask for our opinion. He gives us his orders and he expects them to be obeyed. He’s made known to you the truth and we should act promptly upon the truth that has been made to you, made known to you and I. The duty is clear and the path is clear for us. We should obey it.
