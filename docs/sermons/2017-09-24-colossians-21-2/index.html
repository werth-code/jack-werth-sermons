Colossians chapter 2 verses 1 and 2 this morning for our text and our study. For I would that ye knew what great conflict I have for you, for them at Laudicea, and for as many as have not seen my face in the flesh, that their hearts might be comforted, being knit together in love, and all riches of the fullness, of the full assurance of understanding to the acknowledgement of the mystery of God and of the Father and of Christ. May God bless to us His sacred and His Holy Word this morning. In ancient time, watchtowers were constructed on high hills or mountainsides. for someone to view the area, to be on the lookout for enemy approach, for military observation purposes. And now the watchman in the tower was responsible. Couldn’t fall asleep. He couldn’t be daydreaming up there. He had to be constantly on the lookout that he might see the enemy, shout down below or below the trumpet to warn the troops in the camp so that they would grab their weapons and be ready to take on the enemy. God says that Christian leaders are appointed to be watchmen for the people of God. You remember God told Ezekiel, I have set via watchman for the House of Israel. Paul uses this kind of language when it talks about himself as a watchman for the church. God has set elders in the church. church that they may watch out for your souls, as the Bible indicates to us. And God declares that spiritual leaders will be held accountable, as Paul indicates, I’m free from the blood of all men, because I have declared under you the whole council of God. So Paul sees himself as a watchman for the entire church. He’s careful to alert them to the danger, to the enemy that he sees prowling about. that would trouble the people of God. Now let me begin by saying that true ministers are greatly concerned about your spiritual state. As we see here from verse one, he says, for I would that you knew what great conflict I have for you, for them at Laodosia, and as for as many, as I have not seen my face in the flesh. Ministers, find great conflict in the care. of the flock of God. Paul, having expressed the true message concerning Christ in the gospel in Chapter 1, now turns his attention to dangerous error that’s confronting the church in Chapter 2. As an apostle, Paul’s watch care was especially aroused for those who have not seen him in the flesh. He has not seen him in the flesh. personally preached the gospel to these individuals. But the Colossians, the Laeo Deceans and many other churches established by Papyrus or others, did not hear, Paul actually articulate the message himself. He seems to be concerned that these churches may be more vulnerable to spiritual predators. And Paul sees himself here in a great conflict, a fight, if you would, a contention, a contest of some sort with regard to taking care of the church. As Paul told the Corinthians and said, 2 Corinthians 113. But I fear, less by any means, as Satan begowed E. through his subtlety, so your mind should be corrupted from the simplicity that is in Christ. David relates that while he was watching his father’s sheep out in the fields, he had to be diligent. One time a fearsome lion came and snatched one of the sheep. Another time, there was a hungry bear that did so. And who knows how many predators David had to drive off with his slingshot. And he had to go after the lion and the bear in order to rescue the sheep. sheep that have been carried off. Ministers are called shepherds. They’re called shepherds not only because they are to feed the sheep with the word of God, but they’re also to drive off the predators. They are also to warn the sheep, to protect the sheep. So Paul sees himself in combat, if you would, in conflict with these false teachers who he classifies, you remember, in the book of Acts as wolves, who would come and pray upon the flock of God. And so he must strive and earnestly contend for the faith of the gospel of Christ. But these agents that Satan are trying to carry off professing believers, trying to pull them away from Christ. A minister can’t give place to them, but must defend the truth and refute these evil friends. There are many spiritual predators who are daily threatening to harm God’s people. And we should be aware of this. They appear or could appear as angels of light to deceive you, but actually they are messengers of Satan himself. Paul’s fear was that professing believers would be seduced here. into error, drawn away from the gospel, and perhaps make shipwreck of their faith. So preservation of the flock was of great priority. His goal is to present every man perfect in Christ on that day of judgment in verse 28 of chapter 1. That’s his goal, and he doesn’t want any of them here to be harmed. Ministers must seek to, in some way divert the danger away from the flock and spare the sheep. Now, in order to do that, the minister must first be in perpetual prayer for the safekeeping of the flock. Paul told the Thessalonians, we pray always for you. He told the Philippians, I always make requests for you in all my prayers. And here in chapter 1 and verse 9, for this cause, we also, since the day we heard it, do not cease to pray for you. So he’s been praying for the churches, concerned that God would preserve the people of God. We must realize that ultimately it is God alone that preserves the flock. It keeps your feet from falling. The enemy attacks can be so subtle, so deceptive, so powerful, that no believer would survive were it not for the preservation power of God that not one of his elect would ever be lost. How grateful we should be for Christ intercession as well. And we think about John 1711, Father, Holy Father, keep through thine own name, those whom thou hast given me. Christ prays for his elect that the Father would keep them and that they would not be lost and none of them shall be lost. But it’s the duty of every minister then to be constantly praying for the preservation of the flock, so he’s concerned about this situation. Secondly, your minister must vigorously attack error. A shepherd must be valiant for the truth, as the scripture would indicate to us. Exposing error. Refuting the seducers, expounding the true doctrine, earnestly contending for the faith against any false teacher that would arise.
