You’ve seen town meetings where things get out of hand when the state representative is there and people are screaming and it gets something. Sometimes it gets wild and unruly, disrespectful. We should be respectful while you are firm, perhaps, about our disagreement with the person in charge. James 120 says, The wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God. Your Christian warfare does not need to have a carnal weapon in it. The true faith is one where meekness and humility reign. and 2 Timothy 2.24 and 25 says, The servant of the Lord must not strive but be gentle unto all men, apt to teach, patient, in meekness and instructing those that oppose themselves, if God, peradventure, will give them repentance the acknowledging of the truth. Jesus, when reviled, reviled not again. Paul stated that when we are slandered, we answer softly in 1st Corinthians 413. Proverbs 264 says, answer not a fool according to his folly, lest they’ll be like him. Don’t answer a railing, scoffing fool, just like he has spoken to you. And so these are the kinds of things that we could learn from this passage of scripture tonight. Well, let us close with a word of prayer.
