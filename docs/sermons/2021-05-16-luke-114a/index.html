Luke chapter 11, verses 1 through 4. And it came to pass that as he was praying in a certain place, when he ceased, one of his disciples said unto him, Lord, teach us to pray, as John also taught his disciples. And he said unto them, when ye pray, say, our Father which art in heaven, hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come, thy will be done, as in heaven, so in earth. Give us this day, give us day by day our daily bread, and forgive us our sins, as we also forgive everyone that is indebted. to us, and lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. May God bless to us the reading of His holy and sacred word this morning. The guilt of sin is certainly a very heavy burden for all of us to bear. It’s like carrying a 75 pound weight. on your shoulders. Your guilt can be so intense that you can collapse out of the intense weight of your guilt. Jay Adams is a leading reformed counselor, wrote in his book some startling words in his book, competent to counsel. He wrote in there that he worked with some mental institutions in Indiana. Now, if you look at this carefully, we’ve looked at the first part of this prayer, which had to do with God’s kingdom, the advancement of God’s interest on this earth. The second part of this prayer has to do with our personal interest. You’ll notice here, though, that we should certainly be more careful about our souls than our body. There’s one petition with regard to the body, and that has to do with our daily brain. But you’ll notice there are two petitions with regard to your soul. One, forgiveness of sins, and second, deliverance from evil. So in this model prayer, we have this coming through, that we should be more careful with regard to our soul, than even for our body. We should be more concerned about spiritual matters than even our daily bread. More desirous of your soul’s welfare than just feeding your body. Sadly, I think most people take more care of their body than they do their soul. But for the Christian, Christ emphasizes the need to take care of your soul. Thomas Watson, the old Puritan writer put it this way. Daily bread may make us live comfortably, but forgiveness of sins will make us die comfortably from his body of divinity. Now the focus here, and we have to understand this, is on parental forgiveness. Now, by that I mean that Christ is not referring here to what we’ll call judicial. forgiveness, which is related to your justification and your salvation from sin through Christ. The people who are given this prayer are already disciples of Christ, are already able to call Christ their father. This forgiveness is given after your justification. It involves you and I as disobedient children. of God. The Baptist Confession of Faith is 1689, Chapter 11, paragraph 5 puts it this way. God doth continue to forgive the sins of those who are justified. And although they can never fall from the state of justification, yet they may, by their sins, fall under God’s fatherly displeasure. And in that condition, they have not usually the light of his countenance restored under them until they humble themselves, confess their sins, beg pardon, and renew their faith and repentance. So, to recap, judicial forgiveness is what you receive when God grant you salvation in Christ. Parental forgiveness is what you receive as a child of God. who has been disobedient, and you’ve fallen under God’s fatherly displeasure. So we’re looking at the second aspect here of forgiveness. You remember the story of Christ washing the disciples’ feet? And he came to Peter, and Peter says, are you going to wash my feet? Jesus said, yes, I have to do so. And Peter says, well, Lord, not my feet only, but also on my hands and my head. Do you remember what Jesus said to him? That still needs to be washed by Christ. And so, this is what we could say. After being justified before God, we still get soiled and tarnished in some fashion as we go through this life. So we need this parental forgiveness. Even though you’ve been forgiven in the ultimate sense, by Christ’s work on the cross. It was a picture of how the sins of the people of God would be sent forth, be sent away, be sent away. that they would be forgiven.
